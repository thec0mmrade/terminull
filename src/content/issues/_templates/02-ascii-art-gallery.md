# ASCII Art Gallery: Digital Graffiti

Art has always been central to hacker culture. From the earliest BBS door screens to modern demoscene productions, we express ourselves in the constraints of the terminal.

## The Medium

ASCII art works within the 95 printable characters of the ASCII standard. ANSI art extends this with escape codes for color and positioning. Both demand creativity within constraints -- the hacker way.

## The Art of Constraints

> [!INFO] ASCII art teaches an important lesson: constraints breed creativity. The same principle applies to exploit development, CTF challenges, and elegant code.

Working with a fixed-width grid of characters forces you to think differently about form and negative space. Every character matters. Sound familiar?

## Tools of the Trade

If you want to create your own ASCII art, here are some tools:

* **Monodraw** -- Mac app for ASCII diagrams and art
* **REXPaint** -- Powerful ASCII art editor
* **jp2a** -- Convert images to ASCII
* **figlet** -- Generate text banners
* **toilet** -- Like figlet but with color support

```bash
# Generate a quick banner
figlet -f slant "HACK THE PLANET"

# Convert an image to ASCII
jp2a --width=60 --colors image.png
```
